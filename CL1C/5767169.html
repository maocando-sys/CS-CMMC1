CMMC LVL 1 CS : 2. Scope & System Boundary

Created by Manuel Ocando, last modified on Feb 03, 2026

Purpose

This section defines the scope and system boundaries for the organizationâ€™s CMMC Level 1 (FAR 52.204-21)compliance effort. It identifies the systems, users, and environments that store, process, or transmit Federal Contract Information (FCI) and are therefore subject to CMMC Level 1 requirements.

Only systems and assets explicitly identified as in-scope are subject to assessment.

Definition of Federal Contract Information (FCI)

Federal Contract Information (FCI) is information provided by or generated for the Government under a contract that is not intended for public release and is not classified.

The organization identifies and tracks FCI based on contractual requirements and data handling practices.

In-Scope Systems

The following system categories are in-scope for CMMC Level 1:

  • End-user workstations used by personnel who access FCI

  • Approved cloud services used to store, process, or transmit FCI

  • Identity and access management systems supporting authentication and authorization for in-scope users

  • Network infrastructure and security controls that protect access to in-scope systems

All in-scope systems are managed, configured, and operated in accordance with documented CMMC Level 1 policies and procedures.

In-Scope Users

The following users are in-scope:

  • Employees and contractors who access FCI

  • System administrators responsible for in-scope systems

  • Authorized third parties with approved access to FCI

Access to in-scope systems is limited to authorized users with a documented business need.

Out-of-Scope Systems

The following are considered out-of-scope:

  • Systems that do not store, process, or transmit FCI

  • Personal devices not authorized for access to FCI

  • Business systems that are logically or physically segregated from in-scope environments

Out-of-scope systems are not subject to CMMC Level 1 assessment requirements.

System Boundaries

System boundaries are defined to limit the CMMC Level 1 assessment to approved environments where FCI is handled.

Boundaries are enforced through:

  • Logical access controls

  • Network segmentation

  • Identity and authentication mechanisms

  • Approved communication paths

Unauthorized access across system boundaries is prohibited.

Boundary Changes

Changes to system boundaries, including the introduction of new systems, services, or users that may handle FCI, require review and approval to determine scope impact.

Scope definitions are updated as necessary to reflect approved changes.

Review

This scope and system boundary definition is reviewed:

  • At least annually

  • Upon contract changes involving FCI

  • Upon significant system or architectural changes
